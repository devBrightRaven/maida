Maida User Manual

Maida shows you one game at a time from your Steam library. You answer: play it now, or not now. This manual walks through each screen.

First launch

When you open Maida for the first time, a short onboarding screen appears. It explains what Maida does and what it will read from your device.

Maida reads your locally installed Steam games from your Steam installation directory. No Steam account login is required.

Rin (臨) -- the main view

Rin is where decisions happen. One game appears on screen. Not a list. Not a comparison. Just one.

Two actions

  • Play now -- launch this game immediately through Steam.
  • Not now -- skip this game for this session. It won't appear again until you restart.

That's it. No stars, no tags, no sorting. The game either interests you right now, or it doesn't.

Undo

Made a mistake? Each Not Now decision can be undone. The last skipped game comes back.

Kamae (構) -- narrow the field

Before entering Rin, you can shape the field. Kamae is where you decide what you're willing to face today. Create Kata (groupings) and pick one for the session.

Kata are temporary. You can create one for "short games tonight" or "strategy games this week." They don't persist as permanent categories.

Search in Kamae

If your library is large, search within Kamae to find specific games quickly.

Settings

Maida keeps settings minimal. The three screens below cover everything you can change.

Telemetry

Maida sends one anonymous ping on launch (UUID, days since install, version). You can disable it in Settings. See the Privacy Policy for details.
